1 What is Raiding in Highguard?

Raiding is the core objective of every Highguard match. Unlike traditional shooters where kills determine victory, Highguard requires teams to destroy the enemy's base to win.

Each match follows a cyclical flow: gather resources ‚Üí contest the Shieldbreaker ‚Üí raid the enemy base ‚Üí repeat until one base is destroyed. Understanding this loop is essential for success.

The Raid Cycle

1. Reinforce Phase: Prep your base, pick loadout
2. Gear Up: Explore map, gather weapons/Vesper
3. Shieldbreaker: Contest the magical sword
4. The Raid: Assault or defend base
üí° Key Insight: Matches can last 7-30+ minutes depending on how many raid cycles occur. Each failed raid escalates loot quality, making subsequent fights more intense.

3 Siege Tower Mechanics

After successfully plunging the Shieldbreaker into enemy shields, a Siege Tower spawns near their base. This is your team's foothold for the assault.

Siege Tower Functions

Attackers respawn at the Siege Tower instead of their base, reducing travel time.

Shield Disruption

While the tower stands, enemy base shields remain down, allowing wall breaching.

4 Attacking Strategy

Once the Siege Tower is up, your goal is to destroy the enemy base. There are two paths to victory:

üéØ Option A: Generators

Plant bombs on the base's generators. Each destroyed generator damages the base. Destroying all generators wins the match.

  • ‚úì Can chip damage even if raid fails
  • ‚úó Bombs can be defused by defenders

5 Defending Your Base

Defense is just as important as offense. A well-coordinated defense can stop raids and turn the tide of the match.

1. Reinforce Walls Early

During the Reinforce Phase, fortify key walls. Focus on chokepoints and generator access routes. Reinforced walls take more hits to breach.

2. Use Defensive Wardens

Wardens like Kai (Ice Wall, Frost Giant) and Una (Tree Walker) excel at holding positions. Position them at key intersections.

3. Spread Out Your Defense

Don't stack all defenders at one spot. Cover multiple entry points. Attackers can create diversions while flanking from another angle.

4. Defuse Bombs Immediately

If attackers plant bombs, prioritize defusing over kills. One bomb detonation can turn a successful defense into a loss.

5. Eliminate All Attackers

The raid only ends when all attackers are dead simultaneously or the Siege Tower depletes. Keep pressure until the tower falls.

9 Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Shieldbreaker in Highguard?
The Shieldbreaker is an ancient magical sword that spawns at the center of periodic storms. Teams must fight to claim it, then carry it to the enemy base to initiate raids by weakening their shields.
What does the Siege Tower do?
The Siege Tower spawns after plunging the Shieldbreaker into enemy shields. It serves as a forward respawn point for attackers and keeps enemy shields down until its energy depletes.
How do you win a raid in Highguard?
Win by either planting bombs on and destroying all generators inside the enemy base, or by directly destroying the Anchor Stone for an instant victory.
What happens if a raid fails?
If all attackers are eliminated or the Siege Tower's energy depletes, the raid fails. Enemy shields regenerate, loot quality escalates, and a new Shieldbreaker spawns at a different location.
Which Raid Tool is best for beginners?
The Rocket Launcher is the most versatile choice for beginners. It's effective at destroying walls from a safe distance and can deal damage to enemies in emergencies. The Blast Hammer requires closer range but breaches faster.
Can defenders destroy the Siege Tower?
The Siege Tower has a limited energy pool that depletes over time. Defenders can end a raid by eliminating all attackers before the energy runs out, causing the tower to dissipate and shields to regenerate.
